This former ghost town has been transformed into an exclusive resort that combines rustic wilderness charm with high-end amenities.</p><p>The property features 13 meticulously restored log cabins, each with unique character and modern luxuries. The transformation of this historic site represents one of Colorado's most remarkable preservation stories, maintaining authentic western heritage while offering modern comforts.</p><h3 id="from-mining-town-to-exclusive-resort">From Mining Town to Exclusive Resort</h3><p>Dunton's story began in the late 1800s when prospectors discovered valuable mineral deposits in the San Juan Mountains. By 1885, the settlement had grown into a thriving mining community with over 300 residents seeking their fortunes in gold and silver. The town featured saloons, dance halls, cabins, and a post office—all centered around the natural hot springs that provided relief to miners after long days of labor.</p><p>When mining declined in the early 1900s, Dunton gradually emptied. By the 1990s, it had become a true ghost town with deteriorating structures and fading memories. In 1994, German entrepreneur Christoph Henkel and his wife Katrin purchased the abandoned property and embarked on a painstaking restoration project that took nearly eight years to complete.</p><p>Their vision transformed the ghost town into an exclusive resort while meticulously preserving its historical integrity. Original buildings were carefully dismantled, rebuilt on new foundations, and outfitted with modern amenities while maintaining their rustic character. The renovation included salvaging period materials and artifacts to ensure authenticity throughout the property.</p><h3 id="preserving-colorados-heritage">Preserving Colorado's Heritage</h3><p>The restoration of Dunton Hot Springs stands as a model for heritage conservation in Colorado. Each cabin retains its original character with authentic period furnishings mixed with subtle modern luxuries. The town's layout remains largely unchanged, allowing guests to experience the spatial relationships of the original mining community.</p><p>The owners worked with historical preservationists to document and maintain significant features like the 19th-century saloon with carved names of miners still visible on its wooden walls. Original photographs guided much of the restoration work, ensuring historical accuracy while adapting spaces for contemporary use.</p><p>Beyond the buildings, the preservation efforts extended to documenting the area's cultural heritage. The resort maintains an archive of historical records, photographs, and oral histories from former residents and their descendants. These materials help tell the story of Dunton's past to current visitors through displays throughout the property.</p><p>Local Native American heritage is also honored at Dunton, with acknowledgment of the Ute people who first discovered and used the therapeutic hot springs centuries before European settlement. Traditional names and stories are preserved and shared with guests who visit the springs today.</p><p>The sustainable approach to renovation included using reclaimed materials from the region and implementing energy-efficient systems that minimize environmental impact while maintaining historical aesthetics. This balance between preservation and sustainability makes Dunton Hot Springs a living museum where guests can directly connect with Colorado's mining era while enjoying luxury accommodations.</p><h2 id="accommodations-at-dunton-hot-springs">Accommodations at Dunton Hot Springs</h2><p>Dunton Hot Springs offers 13 distinctive accommodations, each blending historic character with modern comfort.
