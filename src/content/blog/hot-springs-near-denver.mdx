    <p class="section-intro">Denver's location at the foot of the Rockies puts an extraordinary range of geothermal pools within reach. You can be soaking in a 110°F mineral cave in under an hour, or spend a weekend chasing high-alpine pools tucked above timberline.</p>

Use the comparison table to cut straight to what fits your schedule and budget, or read on for full details on each destination.</p>
    <div class="callout">
      <strong>Quick answer:</strong> For the fastest soak, head to <strong>Indian Hot Springs</strong> in Idaho Springs (45 min). For the best all-around experience, <strong>Mount Princeton Hot Springs</strong> (2 hr 15 min) is consistently Colorado's top-ranked resort. For free soaking, <strong>Penny Hot Springs</strong> near Carbondale rewards the drive with zero admission fee.

<section id="under2hrs">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Under 2 Hours <span class="accent">from Denver</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">Three legitimate options for weeknight escapes and spontaneous day trips — no early alarm required.</p>

    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>Indian Hot Springs — Idaho Springs</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>45 minutes</strong> via I-70 West</span>
        <span>💰 <strong>$25–30</strong> day pass</span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>100–112°F</strong></span>
        <span>🏛 Open since 1863</span>
      </div>
      <p>The closest hot spring to Denver is also one of its most distinctive. Indian Hot Springs has operated since 1863, making it one of Colorado's oldest continuously running resorts. The main draw is the underground geothermal caves — tunnels carved into the mountainside where natural hot mineral water flows through rock pools at 100–112°F. It's dark, steamy, and genuinely unusual.</p>
      <p>Above ground, the facility includes a large outdoor swimming pool kept cooler for laps, private indoor baths, and mud baths. The resort shows its age in places, but that's part of the historic character rather than a flaw.</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>Caves fill up on weekend mornings — arrive before 10am or visit weekdays</li>
        <li>The outdoor pool is too cool for winter soaking; stick to the caves October through April</li>
        <li>Combine with a stop at Beau Jo's in Idaho Springs for Colorado-style mountain pizza</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>SunWater Spa — Manitou Springs</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>1 hr 15 min</strong> via I-25 South</span>
        <span>💰 <strong>$28–38</strong> day pass</span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>97–108°F</strong></span>
        <span>🧘 Adults-focused</span>
      </div>
      <p>Manitou Springs sits at the base of Pikes Peak, and SunWater offers a quieter, more wellness-centered experience than the big resort scene. Multiple pools at varying temperatures are terraced into a hillside garden with the Pikes Peak massif as backdrop. The mineral water comes from Manitou's famous springs — the same source that made the town a celebrated 19th-century health resort.</p>
      <p>Pair a visit with Manitou's walkable downtown, Garden of the Gods (15 minutes away), or the Manitou Incline for a full day out.</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>Best suited to adults and couples; not the ideal choice for families with young kids</li>
        <li>Downtown Manitou has excellent independent restaurants for post-soak dinner</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>Hot Sulphur Springs Resort — Hot Sulphur Springs</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>1 hr 45 min</strong> via I-70 / Hwy 40</span>
        <span>💰 <strong>$22–28</strong> day pass</span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>95–112°F</strong></span>
        <span>🛁 21 pools</span>
      </div>
      <p>Hot Sulphur Springs offers excellent value: 21 pools of varying temperatures carved into a hillside above the Colorado River, at one of the more affordable price points among Colorado's developed resorts. This is an authentically Western small-town resort — not a boutique spa — and that's what makes it worth the drive.</p>
      <p>The range of pool temperatures means you can customize your experience from a 95°F lap pool to a 112°F therapy tub. Private pools are available by the hour for those wanting exclusivity without paying full resort rates.</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>One of the best budget options for first-time hot springs visitors</li>
        <li>Private pools book quickly on weekends — call ahead</li>
        <li>The town of Hot Sulphur Springs has limited dining; bring food or plan accordingly</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>


<section id="2to3hrs">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>2–3 Hours <span class="accent">from Denver</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">Colorado's most celebrated resorts — and its best free primitive pools — all fall in this range. This is the sweet spot for day trips and weekend visits.</p>

    <div class="cards-grid" style="margin-bottom:40px;">

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>Mount Princeton</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">⭐ Top Pick</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★★★</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 15 min</span>
            <span>💰 $25–35</span>
            <span>🌡 94–106°F</span>
          </div>
          <p>Colorado's best-regarded hot spring resort. Creekside pools where geothermal water mixes with cold Chalk Creek let you adjust your temperature by moving between pools — the closest thing to a natural experience at a developed resort. The 14,000-foot Collegiate Peaks tower directly above.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Best time:</strong> Fall for peak foliage + ideal temperatures. Creekside pools require summer-fall river levels.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>Cottonwood Hot Springs</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">Budget Gem</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★★☆</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 15 min</span>
            <span>💰 $22–28</span>
            <span>🌡 98–105°F</span>
          </div>
          <p>A quieter, adults-only alternative to Mount Princeton just a few miles away in Buena Vista. Clothing-optional by default; relaxed crowd. Colorado residents get discounted "locals" rates. Easily combined with Mount Princeton for a two-spring day.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Locals rate:</strong> Show Colorado ID for discounted entry.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>Glenwood Hot Springs</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">World's Largest Pool</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★★☆</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 30 min</span>
            <span>💰 $32–40</span>
            <span>🌡 90–104°F</span>
          </div>
          <p>The 405-foot main pool is kept at 90–93°F — genuinely swimmable, with lap lanes, a waterslide, and a diving area. A separate 104°F therapy pool provides the heat option. More pool complex than hot spring retreat, which works perfectly for active families.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Pair with:</strong> Iron Mountain (10 min away) for a two-spring day. Swim caps required for long hair.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>Iron Mountain Hot Springs</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">Most Romantic</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★★★</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 30 min</span>
            <span>💰 $35–45</span>
            <span>🌡 98–108°F</span>
          </div>
          <p>Sixteen intimate pools (2–16 person capacity) terrace down toward the Colorado River, each at a different temperature. Opened in 2015, it represents the modern direction of Colorado hot springs: thoughtful design, upscale amenities, quiet atmosphere. Best for couples.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Book ahead:</strong> Weekend slots fill 2–3 weeks out. Sunset sessions offer the best light.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>South Canyon Springs</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">FREE · Primitive</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★★☆</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 30 min</span>
            <span>💰 Free</span>
            <span>🌡 100–105°F</span>
          </div>
          <p>Three pools on BLM land near Glenwood Springs, reached by a 0.5-mile trail through a narrow canyon. Continuously flowing water keeps conditions cleaner than stagnant springs. Clothing-optional by convention. Year-round access — trail can be icy in winter.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Note:</strong> Access crosses private land — stay on the established trail and pack everything out.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">
          <div>
            <h3>Penny Hot Springs</h3>
            <div class="card-badge">FREE · Roadside</div>
          </div>
          <span class="stars">★★★☆☆</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-body">
          <div class="card-meta">
            <span>⏱ 2 hr 45 min</span>
            <span>💰 Free</span>
            <span>🌡 Varies*</span>
          </div>
          <p>Colorado's most accessible free spring — the rock-lined pools are visible from Hwy 133 alongside the Crystal River. Temperature varies dramatically with river flow; peak conditions run August through October when cold river water mixes with the 130°F+ source.</p>
          <div class="card-tips"><strong>Best window:</strong> August–October. Parking fills by mid-morning on weekends — arrive early.</div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    
    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>Strawberry Park Hot Springs — Steamboat Springs</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>3 hours</strong> via I-70 / Hwy 40</span>
        <span>💰 <strong>$25 cash only</strong></span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>101–106°F</strong></span>
        <span>🌲 Adults-only after dark</span>
      </div>
      <p>Strawberry Park delivers the most beautiful developed hot spring setting in Colorado. Stone-lined pools of varying temperatures blend into a forested hillside, with Hot Springs Creek running alongside. In winter, steam rises through snow-covered trees creating a scene that feels genuinely wild rather than constructed.</p>
      <p>After dark, the springs become clothing-optional and adults-only (18+), which changes the atmosphere considerably from the family-friendly daytime hours. Water temperatures run 101–106°F in the main pools with a cold plunge option.</p>
      <p>The access challenge is real: the final 4 miles are unpaved and become treacherous in winter without 4WD or chains. The resort runs a shuttle from Steamboat Springs ($10 round trip) if conditions are bad.</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>Cash only — no cards accepted at all</li>
        <li>No reservations for day use; arrive early on weekends, the lot fills</li>
        <li>Cabins available for overnight stays but book months in advance for peak season</li>
        <li>Don't attempt the access road in snow without proper traction — it's genuinely dangerous</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>Old Town Hot Springs — Steamboat Springs</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>3 hours</strong> via I-70 / Hwy 40</span>
        <span>💰 <strong>$28–35</strong> day pass</span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>98–106°F</strong></span>
        <span>🏊 Waterslides + climbing wall</span>
      </div>
      <p>Old Town Hot Springs is Steamboat's rec-center style facility — less about serene soaking and more about active fun for all ages. The facility includes waterslides, a climbing wall, lap pool, and traditional hot pools. It's the right choice when you're visiting Steamboat with kids who need more than a soak to stay entertained.</p>
      <p>As a bonus, Old Town sits in downtown Steamboat, so you can walk to restaurants and shops before or after your visit. Combine with Strawberry Park for a complete Steamboat hot springs day (very different vibes, complementary experiences).</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>Best family option in the Steamboat area</li>
        <li>Lap swim lanes available for those wanting fitness alongside the soak</li>
        <li>Steamboat's ski area is minutes away — ideal for ski-and-soak winter trips</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

<section id="3plus">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>3+ Hours <span class="accent">from Denver</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">These destinations require a full weekend commitment, but the San Juan Mountains deliver some of Colorado's most spectacular hot spring settings.</p>

    <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:24px; margin-bottom:28px;">
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>Ouray Hot Springs</h3>
        <div class="spring-meta-bar" style="flex-direction:column; gap:4px;">
          <span>⏱ <strong>4 hours</strong> · 💰 <strong>$16–22</strong> · 🌡 <strong>96–106°F</strong></span>
        </div>
        <p>The town-run facility in Ouray — the "Switzerland of America" — offers an affordable, family-focused experience with a main swimming area, waterslide, and dedicated soaking pools. The surrounding San Juan peaks are staggering. Combine with Ouray's famous ice climbing or jeep roads in summer.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>Orvis Hot Springs</h3>
        <div class="spring-meta-bar" style="flex-direction:column; gap:4px;">
          <span>⏱ <strong>4 hours</strong> · 💰 <strong>$20–30</strong> · 🌡 <strong>98–112°F</strong></span>
        </div>
        <p>A clothing-optional adults-only resort near Ridgway with a devoted following. Several outdoor pools plus an indoor soaking room; the outdoor pond pool is the signature feature. Quieter and more intimate than most Colorado resorts. Overnight lodging available.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:24px;">
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>Pagosa Hot Springs</h3>
        <div class="spring-meta-bar" style="flex-direction:column; gap:4px;">
          <span>⏱ <strong>4 hr 15 min</strong> · 💰 <strong>$30–45</strong> · 🌡 <strong>95–114°F</strong></span>
        </div>
        <p>Pagosa Springs sits atop the world's deepest known hot spring, and two competing resorts — The Springs Resort and Healing Waters — let you choose your experience level. The San Juan River runs between the pools and the hot spring source. Worth the drive for a full weekend in the southern San Juans.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>Dunton Hot Springs</h3>
        <div class="spring-meta-bar" style="flex-direction:column; gap:4px;">
          <span>⏱ <strong>5.5 hours</strong> · 💰 <strong>Guests only</strong> · 🌡 <strong>106°F</strong></span>
        </div>
        <p>A restored 1800s ghost town turned ultra-luxury lodge in the San Juans. The hot springs are exclusive to overnight guests — this is not a day-visit destination. At $1,000+/night, it's Colorado's most exclusive hot spring experience, and the remote Dolores River Canyon setting is extraordinary.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>


<section id="free">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Free Hot Springs <span class="accent">Near Denver</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">Colorado has more free geothermal pools than most people realize. These three require no admission — just the drive, some effort, and leave-no-trace respect.</p>

    <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:24px; margin-bottom:28px;">
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>Penny Hot Springs</h3>
        <p>Alongside the Crystal River on Hwy 133 south of Carbondale. Rock-lined pools maintained by volunteers where 130°F+ source water mixes with the cold river. No facilities — nearest restroom is in Redstone. Best August–October when river levels drop. Pack everything out.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="spring-entry" style="margin-bottom:0;">
        <h3>South Canyon Hot Springs</h3>
        <p>Three BLM pools near Glenwood Springs at a steady 100–105°F, reached by a half-mile trail. Continuously flowing water keeps conditions cleaner than most primitive springs. Clothing-optional convention. Year-round, though icy in winter. Stay on the established trail — access crosses private land.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="spring-entry">
      <h3>Conundrum Hot Springs — Alpine Backcountry</h3>
      <div class="spring-meta-bar">
        <span>⏱ <strong>3 hr drive + 8.5-mile hike</strong></span>
        <span>💰 <strong>Free</strong> (camping permit required)</span>
        <span>🌡 <strong>102–104°F</strong></span>
        <span>⛰ 11,200 ft elevation</span>
      </div>
      <p>The crown jewel of Colorado backcountry soaking — and the hardest to reach. Alpine pools at 11,200 feet elevation, surrounded by 14,000-foot peaks in the Maroon Bells-Snowmass Wilderness. The main pool holds 6–8 people with views extending to Castle Peak. The tradeoff is a 17-mile round trip with 2,500 feet of elevation gain; most visitors turn it into an overnight backpack.</p>
      <ul class="tips-list">
        <li>Wilderness camping permit required for overnight stays — check the Aspen Ranger District for current requirements</li>
        <li>Snow-free access typically runs July through September</li>
        <li>Bear canisters required for food storage</li>
        <li>Carry all water; the spring minerals make it unsuitable for drinking</li>
        <li>Crowded on summer weekends despite the effort required — go on weekdays if possible</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="callout">
      <strong>Free spring rules:</strong> Pack out all trash including food scraps. Don't rearrange rocks that others have placed. Keep groups small. Clothing-optional is the default at primitive springs — don't make it awkward for others. These springs stay open because visitors treat them well.
    </div>
  </div>
</section>


<section id="family">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Family-Friendly <span class="accent">Hot Springs Guide</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">Not all hot springs welcome kids, and not all kids can handle sitting in 104°F water. Here's how to plan a successful family outing.</p>

    <div class="family-grid" style="margin-bottom:28px;">
      <div class="family-box go">
        <h4>✓ Best for Families</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Glenwood Hot Springs</strong> — waterslide, swimable temps, diving area. Best overall for kids.</li>
          <li><strong>Mount Princeton</strong> — creekside pools let kids explore and self-regulate temperature.</li>
          <li><strong>Old Town Hot Springs</strong> — waterslides, climbing wall, lap pool. Very activity-oriented.</li>
          <li><strong>Ouray Hot Springs</strong> — town-run, affordable, family-focused pool complex.</li>
          <li><strong>Hot Sulphur Springs</strong> — range of pool temps, good value, accessible for all ages.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="family-box skip">
        <h4>✗ Skip with Young Children</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Strawberry Park</strong> — adults-only after dark, remote access road.</li>
          <li><strong>Orvis Hot Springs</strong> — clothing-optional, adults-only pools.</li>
          <li><strong>Cottonwood Hot Springs</strong> — adult-oriented atmosphere.</li>
          <li><strong>All primitive springs</strong> — no facilities, uncontrolled temperatures, no lifeguards.</li>
          <li><strong>Conundrum</strong> — 17-mile hike, high elevation, serious backcountry conditions.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="tips-grid">
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">⏱</span>
        <h4>Limit Soak Time for Kids</h4>
        <p>Keep young children to 15–20 minutes per soak in pools above 98°F. The cooler main pool at Glenwood (90–93°F) allows extended use.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🌡</span>
        <h4>Stick to Cooler Pools</h4>
        <p>Pools under 100°F are safest for children. Skip the therapy pools (104°F+) with kids under 12.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">💧</span>
        <h4>Hydrate More Than You Think</h4>
        <p>Hot water dehydrates children faster than adults realize. Bring at least one water bottle per child and take breaks every 20 minutes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🌙</span>
        <h4>Avoid Evening Hours</h4>
        <p>Many resorts shift to adults-only or clothing-optional after 6–8pm. Check policies before planning an evening visit with kids.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

        <p style="font-size:13px; color:var(--muted); margin-bottom:10px;">Steamboat Springs · 3 hours north</p>

Pair with Old Town Hot Springs for a day two contrast — totally different vibes. Steamboat's downtown restaurants and, in winter, world-class skiing fill out the weekend.</p>
        <p style="font-size:13px; color:var(--muted); margin-top:10px;"><em>4WD or chains required in winter for the access road, or take the resort shuttle.</em></p>

        <p style="font-size:13px; color:var(--muted); margin-bottom:10px;">Buena Vista / Salida · 2 hr 15 min southwest</p>
        <p style="font-size:15px;">Stay in Buena Vista or artsy Salida and work through the area's hot spring cluster: Mount Princeton on day one, Cottonwood on day two. In between: the Arkansas River's Class IV whitewater, mountain biking, Collegiate Peaks hiking, and Salida's genuinely good restaurant scene.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

Add Glenwood Caverns Adventure Park for a full weekend with zero repetition.</p>

<section id="season">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Best Time to Visit <span class="accent">Colorado Hot Springs</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">Hot springs are a year-round activity — but each season changes the experience significantly.</p>
    <div class="season-grid" style="margin-bottom:32px;">
      <div class="season-block">
        <div class="season-name">Winter</div>
        <div class="season-rating">★★★★</div>
        <div class="season-note">Snow falling into steaming pools is peak Colorado. Expect higher prices, crowds, and required reservations. Road conditions complicate remote access.</div>
      </div>
      <div class="season-block">
        <div class="season-name">Spring</div>
        <div class="season-rating">★★★☆</div>
        <div class="season-note">Lighter crowds and lower rates. Primitive springs often flooded by snowmelt — stick to developed resorts. Unpredictable weather.</div>
      </div>
      <div class="season-block">
        <div class="season-name">Summer</div>
        <div class="season-rating">★★★★</div>
        <div class="season-note">Best for free primitive springs as rivers drop and trails clear. Developed resorts stay busy with tourists. Some pools feel too warm on hot days.</div>
      </div>
      <div class="season-block">
        <div class="season-name">Fall</div>
        <div class="season-rating">★★★★★</div>
        <div class="season-note">Best overall season. Aspen colors, stable weather, ideal primitive spring conditions, moderate crowds. Book popular weekends well ahead.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="callout">
      <strong>The verdict:</strong> Fall (September–November) is the sweet spot — free springs at peak conditions, resorts at manageable crowds, and mountain scenery at its annual high point.
    </div>
  </div>
</section>


<section id="tips">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>What to Bring <span class="accent">& Etiquette</span></h2>
    <p class="section-intro">A few things that separate a great hot springs day from a frustrating one.</p>

    <div class="pack-grid" style="margin-bottom:40px;">
      <div class="pack-box">
        <h4>🏨 Developed Resorts</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Swimsuit</li>
          <li>Towel (rentals usually available)</li>
          <li>Water bottle — 32oz minimum per person</li>
          <li>Flip-flops for walking between pools</li>
          <li>Cash for lockers at some locations</li>
          <li>Reservation confirmation on your phone</li>
          <li>Sunscreen for outdoor pools</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="pack-box">
        <h4>🌿 Primitive Springs</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Swimsuit (optional at most)</li>
          <li>Quick-dry towel</li>
          <li>Water shoes for rocky pools</li>
          <li>Trash bag for pack-out</li>
          <li>Water and snacks</li>
          <li>First aid basics</li>
          <li>Headlamp if visiting near dark</li>
          <li>GPS coordinates downloaded offline</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h3 style="margin-bottom:16px;">Etiquette That Matters</h3>
    <div class="tips-grid">
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🚿</span>
        <h4>Shower Before Entering</h4>
        <p>At developed resorts, showering before the pool is standard and often required. Rinse off sunscreen, lotion, and sweat.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🔇</span>
        <h4>Keep Voices Down</h4>
        <p>Soaking areas are for quiet relaxation. Conversations at normal volume carry far over water. People didn't drive three hours to hear your speakerphone.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🧹</span>
        <h4>Pack Out Everything</h4>
        <p>At primitive springs, pack out all trash including food scraps and bottle caps. The free springs stay free because visitors maintain them.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">💵</span>
        <h4>Carry Cash</h4>
        <p>Strawberry Park is cash-only. Several primitive springs have fee parking. Some locker rooms are cash-only. Keep $40 on hand.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🪨</span>
        <h4>Don't Move Rocks</h4>
        <p>At primitive springs, other visitors built those pool configurations. Rearranging rocks changes temperatures and conditions for everyone. Leave it as you found it.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <span class="tip-emoji">🚗</span>
        <h4>Check Road Conditions</h4>
        <p>Strawberry Park's unpaved access road is genuinely dangerous in snow without 4WD. Conundrum is snowbound until July. Check CDOT before heading out.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
